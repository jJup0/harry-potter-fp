# FP Scoring Rules (Character Faithfulness)

## What FP Measures

FP measures ONE thing only: **how faithful the character is in the films compared to the books.**

FP does NOT measure:

- Importance
- Screen time
- Charisma
- Actor quality
- Whether you like them more or less

A character can score 100 even with minimal screen time, if what little they have is faithful.

## Structure

FP = sum of 4 dimensions, each scored 0-25. Total: 0-100.

1. Personality
2. Narrative Role
3. Motivations
4. Character Arc

Each dimension ALWAYS justified with:

- Books (evidence)
- Films (evidence)
- Direct comparison

---

## 1. Personality (0-25)

**What it evaluates:** Whether the character's personality (how they think, speak, act) is the same.

### In the BOOKS, analyze

- Dominant traits (brave, cruel, insecure, ironic...)
- Usual emotional tone
- How they react to conflict
- Canonical internal contradictions
- Look at the OVERALL PATTERN, not isolated scenes

### In the FILMS, analyze

- Whether those traits are preserved
- Whether important flaws are removed
- Whether traits are added that don't exist in the books
- Whether they become more passive/aggressive without basis

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 23-25 | Personality practically identical |
| 18-22 | Minor changes (simplification, softening) |
| 12-17 | Clear character changes |
| 6-11 | Personality altered |
| 0-5 | Character unrecognizable |

**Examples:** Hermione losing her insecurity = penalty. Ron losing his initiative = penalty. McGonagall intact = no penalty.

---

## 2. Narrative Role (0-25)

**What it evaluates:** What the character's PURPOSE is in the story, not how much they appear.

### In the BOOKS, analyze

- Structural function (mentor, antagonist, comic relief, plot driver...)
- Weight in important decisions
- Whether they cause changes in other characters

### In the FILMS, analyze

- Whether that function is maintained
- Whether their actions are transferred to another character
- Whether they get artificial added prominence
- Whether they're reduced to decoration

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 23-25 | Role identical |
| 18-22 | Role reduced but recognizable |
| 12-17 | Role altered |
| 6-11 | Role substituted |
| 0-5 | Role eliminated |

**Golden rule:** If another character does what they did in the books, there IS a penalty.

---

## 3. Motivations (0-25)

**What it evaluates:** WHY the character acts.

### In the BOOKS, analyze

- Explicit objectives
- Internal desires
- Fears
- Moral conflicts

### In the FILMS, analyze

- Whether those motivations are still present
- Whether they're simplified ("just because")
- Whether they're changed for script convenience
- Whether they're poorly explained but still the same

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 23-25 | Motivations intact |
| 18-22 | Simplified but correct |
| 12-17 | Motivations altered |
| 6-11 | Motivations wrong |
| 0-5 | Motivations opposite |

**Important:** Poorly explained does not equal different. Only penalize if they CHANGE.

---

## 4. Character Arc (0-25)

**What it evaluates:** Whether the character's evolution is the same.

### In the BOOKS, analyze

- Starting point
- Internal changes
- Final resolution
- Whether the arc is flat (and should be)

### In the FILMS, analyze

- Whether the arc exists
- Whether it's accelerated
- Whether it's eliminated
- Whether a new one is invented

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 23-25 | Arc complete and faithful |
| 18-22 | Arc compressed |
| 12-17 | Arc incomplete |
| 6-11 | Arc altered |
| 0-5 | Arc nonexistent or false |

**Important rule:** A character can score 25 if their arc is flat in the books AND flat in the films.

---

## Final Calculation

FP = P + R + M + A

- Range: 0-100
- Time, mentions, and screen presence do NOT factor in
- Two characters with different importance CAN have the same FP

---

## Non-Negotiable Golden Rules

1. NEVER compare characters against each other
2. NEVER use "looks better" or subjective quality
3. NEVER use screen time as a factor
4. ALWAYS justify with specific book and film evidence
5. If there is no change, there is no penalty
6. Little presence does not equal little faithfulness
